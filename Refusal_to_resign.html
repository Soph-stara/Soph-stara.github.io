(Mad) Hope

← Back

(Mad) Hope is a kinetic textile installation that stages the tension between entropy and resistance. Using robotic motors to repeatedly stretch and release fabric, the work makes tangible the oscillation between resignation (release) and the refusal to resign (stretch).

Concept

The Second Law of Thermodynamics tells us that entropy (disorder) will always increase in an isolated system. Yet hope persists. The artist approaches this contradiction not as something to resolve, but as a state to inhabit. Drawing on Schrödinger's concept of negentropy, the installation enacts resistance through continuous energy input: motors function as agents of order, temporarily defying material collapse.

The work asks: why would we hope, why would we want to live, when everything will eventually decay? The answer is not comfort or resolution, but a beautiful madness— "Dum Spiro, Spero" (While I breathe, I hope). This act of defiance, of remaining within contradiction rather than resolving it, is what animates the piece.

Installation

The textile structure is mounted diagonally on the wall at approximately two meters, tilting downward. Viewers stand directly beneath the suspended installation, placing themselves within its field of movement rather than observing from a distance.

→ DC or micro servo motors provide sustained mechanical force
→ The fabric gradually shows signs of strain and fatigue
→ The system remains intentionally unstable and imperfect
→ Audible and tactile: friction, mechanical whirring, tightening
→ Spatial arrangement reinforces precarity and endurance

Experience

The rhythmic movement evokes breathing or a heartbeat—life as an ongoing, energy-dependent condition. The angled suspension generates bodily awareness of looming pressure, as if the system could collapse at any moment. Through this tension between mechanical persistence and material vulnerability, the viewer experiences cognitive dissonance: the knowledge that decay is inevitable, and yet the system continues.

Part of Hope4Hope, a group exhibition at Donaufestival 2026. As global crises multiply and exhaustion becomes chronic, MA students from the Department of Cross-Disciplinary Strategies at the University of Applied Arts Vienna undertake a search for traces of hope across various fields, exploring the conditions under which hope manifests.
