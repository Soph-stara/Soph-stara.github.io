(Mad) Hope is an interactive, generative system that stages a fragile tension between entropy and order. The work visualizes how disorder spreads autonomously, while moments of care and attention require constant presence.

Concept

The piece treats entropy as an active force that propagates without intention, while hope emerges only through situated, embodied intervention. The user’s cursor functions as a temporary agent of order, producing small, unstable islands of structure.

Interaction

→ The system evolves continuously
→ Entropy spreads through local diffusion
→ Order requires sustained proximity
→ Withdrawal leads inevitably to decay
