Mapping Commons is an artistic research project that investigates stickers as indicators of places of commons and practices of commoning in urban space. The project is inspired by Silvia Federici’s feminist framework of the commons.

For this work, I photographed and catalogued over 100 stickers found on street signs and traffic lights in the area surrounding my apartment. Each sticker was documented with attention to its origin, iconography, political message, and spatial context.

From this material, I created a structured dataset and programmed an interactive map visualizing the geographic distribution and connections between sticker-based commoning practices.

Methodologically, the project combines ethnographic observation, digital mapping, and artistic research to make visible forms of collective infrastructure that often remain unnoticed in everyday life.
