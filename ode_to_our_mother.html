Ode to Our Mother

We write to the ground beneath us,
to the water remembering our names,
to the air carrying our excess.

This project is a poetic reflection on care,
extraction, and interdependence.

Ode to Our Mother is an artistic research project that seeks to evoke reverence for nature by foregrounding human dependence on natural ecosystems. It draws connections between poetic insight and visual representation, inviting a contemplative engagement with ecological relations.

The research was conducted through the combination of poetry and nature photography, creating a visual–literary dialogue that guides viewers through the poems while preserving space for personal interpretation. Rather than illustrating the text directly, the images operate alongside the poems as parallel modes of sensing and meaning-making.

Within this project, I developed a conceptual framework in which photographs function as interpretative lenses. They render the poems more approachable while encouraging reflection on ecological, emotional, and relational themes. Together, text and image form an invitation to slow down, attend, and respond with care.

In devotion.

Sophie Hamann
