Sound of Drugs

← Back

Sound of Drugs is an artistic research project that explores how biochemical properties can be translated into sound. Rather than attempting to reproduce or simulate drug-induced states, the project investigates data sonification as a method for interpreting altered states through structured, analogue mappings.

A custom dataset was developed covering eight psychoactive substances, incorporating chemical, physiological, and experiential variables. These data profiles inform a data-to-sound translation system in which drug characteristics are mapped to sonic parameters.

Sound Production & Method

The sound compositions were generated using Sonic Pi, a live-coding environment based on the Ruby programming language. For each substance, generative sound pieces were algorithmically produced based on its specific data profile, treating sound as an analytical and expressive medium rather than a vehicle for sensory imitation.
