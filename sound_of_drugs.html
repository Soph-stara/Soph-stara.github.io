Sound of Drugs

← Back

The project investigates the relationship between data sonification and altered states of consciousness. Instead of attempting to simulate or imitate drug-induced highs through sound, the work explores how drugs themselves can be translated into sound via an analogue data-driven system.

A custom dataset was developed covering eight psychoactive substances, incorporating chemical, physiological, and experiential variables. These data profiles form the basis for algorithmic sound generation.

Sound Production

The sound compositions were generated using Sonic Pi, a live-coding environment based on the Ruby programming language. Sonic Pi was used to algorithmically generate generative sound pieces for each substance, informed directly by its specific data profile.

Audio Drugs vs. This Project

While commercial platforms such as i-Doser offer binaural beat compositions that aim to imitate the effects of drugs like THC or MDMA, this project takes a fundamentally different approach. Rather than simulating a high, Sound of Drugs translates drug properties into sound through structured data mappings, emphasizing interpretation over imitation.
