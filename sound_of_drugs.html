Sound of Drugs

← Back

The project investigates the relationship between data sonification and altered states of consciousness. Instead of attempting to simulate or imitate drug-induced highs through sound, the work explores how drugs themselves can be translated into sound via an analogue data-driven system.

A custom dataset was developed covering eight psychoactive substances, incorporating chemical, physiological, and experiential variables. These data profiles form the basis for algorithmic sound generation.

Sound Production & Method

The sound compositions were generated using Sonic Pi, a live-coding environment based on the Ruby programming language. For each substance, a data-to-sound translation system was developed, mapping chemical, physiological, and experiential variables to sonic parameters in order to algorithmically generate generative sound pieces informed by each substance’s specific data profile.

Rather than simulating a high, Sound of Drugs translates drug properties into sound through structured data mappings, emphasizing interpretation over imitation. This approach frames sound as an analytical and expressive medium, foregrounding process, structure, and relationality over sensory replication.
